 In Tanzania, as of April 2026, the **Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC)** and the **Bank of Tanzania (BoT)** have moved into full enforcement of the 2022 Act and new Cybersecurity Guidelines.

Because you are sharing interest (revenue) from bank float, your Terms & Conditions (T&C) must protect you from being classified as an "unlicensed deposit-taker" or an "unregulated investment scheme."

### 1. Key Legal & Regulatory Risks (Tanzania Context)
* **Data Residency:** Financial data must technically be stored or "mirrored" within Tanzania. Your T&C must specify that data is processed in compliance with the **Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) 2022**.
* **The "Interest" Label:** Avoid calling the 1% "Interest." In Tanzania, paying "Interest" often requires a specific banking license. Call it a **"Developer Reward,"** **"Platform Rebate,"** or **"Liquidity Commission."**
* **Anti-Money Laundering (AML):** Since you are a gateway to Ecobank, you are legally a "Reporting Entity." You must have the right to suspend any developer account immediately if the BoT or Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) flags their traffic.

Give this prompt to a developer or an AI to generate the first draft of your legal docs.

> **Prompt:** "Draft a comprehensive Terms and Conditions document for **Safari API**, a payment infrastructure platform operated by **ShuleSoft Ltd** in partnership with **Ecobank Tanzania**.
>

>    * **The Threshold Rule:** Explicitly state that rewards only accrue once the daily average float exceeds **TZS 500,000,000**. Amounts below this threshold earn 0%.
>    * **Payout Logic:** Rewards accrue daily and are paid monthly into the developer’s wallet.
> 3. **The Two-Tier Integration Policy:**
>    * **Condition A (Independent Software Vendors/Developers):** Software companies integrating Safari API for multiple third-party clients are eligible for the Liquidity Reward.
>    * **Condition B (Direct Organizations):** If an organization integrates the API for its own internal use (Direct Integration), neither the organization nor its internal developers are eligible for the Liquidity Reward. The reward is strictly an incentive for **Third-Party Integrators (Aggregators).**
> 4. **Data Protection (TZ PDPA 2022):**
>    * Confirm all personal data is processed according to Tanzanian law.
>    * Include a 'Data Processing Agreement' (DPA) clause where the developer acts as the Data Processor and Safari API/Ecobank as the Data Controller.
> 5. **Compliance & Audit:**
>    * ShuleSoft reserves the right to perform KYC (Know Your Customer) and KYB (Know Your Business) checks.
>    * Immediate termination rights for suspected fraud, money laundering, or 'gaming' the float system.

### 3. Strategy for the "Organization vs. Developer" Rule
To keep your partnerships stable and avoid friction, you must be very clear about *why* you have two conditions. 

* **The Psychological Reason:** If you pay a company’s internal developer a 1% commission, you are creating a **Conflict of Interest** for that employee. The company will eventually find out and feel you are "bribing" their staff.
* **The "Agency" Model:** Frame the 1% as a **"Channel Partner Commission."** * **Direct Org:** "You get the best-in-class infrastructure for free (0% fees)."
    * **Dev House:** "You get the infrastructure for free **PLUS** a commission for managing the liquidity of your clients."

### Final Advice: The "Partner Portal"
In your dashboard, when a user signs up, ask one simple question:
**"Are you integrating for your own company or for a client?"**

If they select **"For a Client,"** they see the 1% potential. If they select **"For my Company,"** they see the "0% Transaction Fee" benefit. This prevents the "developers demanding more" problem because you’ve categorized them correctly from day one.
